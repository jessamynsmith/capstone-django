Welcome to the Global Inland Water Explorer!


WHAT? The Global Inland Water Explorer is a tool for investigating trends in inland surface water. Underlying the interface is a spatial database that stores almost two million polygons representing the Earth’s largest water bodies, from 2000 to 2015. To learn more about the data used, please see the About section.

HOW? To explore the database, you can search by lake name or geographical region. For the former, attribute information about the selected lake will be shown. If searching by location, statistics on the group of water bodies will be returned. This search type also enables the option to filter by surface area size or water body type.

WHY? As climate change intensifies and surface water extent changes, uncertainty regarding water availability is greater than ever. Over two billion people are already living in “water-stressed” regions and the overall amount of freshwater that is available for irrigation and consumption is expected to decrease. Because climate change will continue to play a large role in the changing hydrological cycle, it is important that researchers are able to monitor how water bodies are transforming over time on a regional and individual basis.
